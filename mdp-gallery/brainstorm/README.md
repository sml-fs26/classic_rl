## What was cut and why

Cut the redundant maintenance/operations near-duplicates to protect bucket diversity: machine-last-mile and drift-control are the same run/repair/replace staircase as the chosen machine-maintenance; crunch-mode and surge-desk are weaker staffing variants of the same escalating-threshold idea. Dropped restock-roulette despite its clean (s,S) result (9/8/8) because the inventory dimension is already covered by critical-spare and the customer-growth bucket needed strengthening; free-trial-conversion's richer two-axis flip won that seat. Among pricing/revenue variants I kept only the strongest of each sub-mechanic and cut coffee-cart (vis 7), overbooking, ad-bidding, subscription-upsell, support-winback-triage, marketing-budget-funnel, and sell-the-house (a fine optimal-stopping story, but its 1D reservation-price drop is less visually striking than the 2D flips kept). storm-route and elevator-parking are solid but lower-scoring time/weather flips already represented in spirit. frozen-lake-penguin was dropped in favor of windy-treasure-grid: same gridworld idea, but its own note flags a weaker twist with sparse holes and windy scores higher on vis (10 vs 9) and ped (9 vs 7). No strong HR/people candidate exists in the slate, so that bucket was deliberately left out rather than forcing a low-scoring pick that would hurt manager appeal, visual cleanliness, and twist richness; the final 12 balance evenly across revenue (4), operations (4), and classic textbook/playful (4), satisfying both hard requirements (pig-dice as the simplified-blackjack hit/stand family, plus recycling-robot and gamblers-problem as canonical Sutton & Barto MDPs).
