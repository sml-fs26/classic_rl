SML · Reinforcement Learning

Classic RL visualizations

A short curriculum: from the MDP framework, through the explore-exploit dilemma and the Bellman recursion, to a SARSA agent learning a small cliff-walk by itself, and finally a pair of integrative reviews — Snakes & Ladders for the abstract board, a classic Pokemon battle for the cultural one. Below the curriculum, a manager-facing gallery reframes the same RL bones (MDP, policy, return, Q*, Bellman, DP, SARSA) as everyday business decisions, for an audience with little programming background. Each viz is a single click-step page; press t for theme, ←/→ to navigate scenes.

  1. ANYmal MDP — first contact

    Manually drive ANYmal on a 4×7 grid. State, action, transition, reward — the four operational pieces of an MDP, named on screen.

    open →

  2. Casino — explore / exploit

    Five slot machines, hidden win probabilities. Pull manually, then watch ε-greedy and decaying ε learn to balance trying things against using what's been learned.

    open →

  3. Spooky House — Bellman

    A 5×5 grid with per-cell rewards. Compute the optimal value function with one backward sweep, read the policy off V, then add γ and watch the path bend.

    open →

  4. Darts in the dark — Robbins-Monro

    Throw darts at a target you can't see. Track an estimate that updates online, and feel the trade-off between learning rate and stability.

    open →

  5. SARSA — the capstone

    The four prior pieces fuse into one update rule. Watch a 21-state Q-table fill in across episodes on a small column-patrol cliff-walk.

    open →

  6. Snakes & Ladders — integrative review

    A children’s game is an MDP. The whole curriculum, replayed on one board: roll dice manually, watch value iteration converge on cycles, slide γ, then let SARSA learn it from scratch.

    open →

  7. Pokemon battle — a wild CHARMANDER appeared!

    A wild CHARMANDER appeared! Same RL lesson, classic Pokemon palette. Pikachu battles Charmander; Bellman, γ, ε-greedy and SARSA each play their role under a Gen-1 Red/Blue battle screen.

    open →

  8. Manager gallery: Last-Minute Pricing

    Perishable stock, a midnight deadline. Each day you set a price (premium, standard, or fire-sale), the demand draw decides how many sell, and unsold units expire worthless. The best price flips across a board of inventory by days-left: the whole revenue-management surface as an MDP, learned by DP and then by SARSA.

    open →

  9. Manager gallery: Press Your Luck

    Roll to grow a pot or bank it before a 1 wipes it out, racing a rival to the finish. The optimal call flips on the scoreboard: push your luck when behind, protect the lead when ahead. The Pig dice game as an MDP, with the same DP-then-SARSA arc.

    open →
